Launch into Computing

An introduction to computing concepts, methodologies, and professional practice.

Learning Outcomes

This module introduced the foundational principles of computing and examined how computing systems operate within broader socio-technical contexts, where technology, human behaviour, organisational structures, and societal values interact (Walton, 2024). It explored core computing disciplines including software engineering, data science, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and enterprise IT, highlighting their role in enabling secure, scalable, and ethical digital systems.

By completing this module, I developed the ability to critically analyse computing challenges in business contexts, synthesise academic and industry sources, and articulate the legal, ethical, social, and professional considerations that influence computing design and security decisions.

Reflection

This module strengthened my understanding of computing as more than a technical discipline, reinforcing the importance of governance, ethics, and human factors in system design and cybersecurity. I gained confidence in applying academic Bodies of Knowledge to real-world contexts and learned the importance of engaging critically with peer perspectives to refine arguments.
Overall, the module enhanced my analytical thinking, academic writing discipline, and ability to connect computing theory with professional cybersecurity practice.
